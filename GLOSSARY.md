# Glossary

## archetype simplex

A cognitive or symbolic simplex that holds deep form-pattern possibility. Application-layer term, not root.

## boundary

A distinction-defined simplex role. Boundary is simplex of distinction.

## boundary-simplex

A simplex whose state modifies passage, relation, gradient dynamics, vector direction, or transformation outcome.

## concept simplex

A cognitive or symbolic simplex that holds stable category. Application-layer term, not root.

## consciousness

One possible observer-mode. Consciousness is not required for time, registration, object-instantiation, legibility, readability, or Energy Theory root operation.

## distinction

Legible difference.

## domain

A field of application or explication, such as physics, mathematics, linguistics, thermodynamics, chemistry, literature, psychology, computation, or social systems. Domains may provide tools; domains may not define root.

## energy-state

Energy in a state of being.

## explicationary tool

A borrowed or domain-shaped term that clarifies Energy Theory without owning or altering the root.

## gestalt simplex

Cognitive registration of integrated object-whole. Gestalt simplex is not the source of physical object existence.

## gradient

Readable difference within relation.

## gradient dynamics

Directional behavior of readable differences. Gradient dynamics are how vectors select direction through tensor arrangement, simplex relations, and boundary-simplex states.

## idea simplex

A cognitive or symbolic simplex that holds active transformable thought. Application-layer term, not root.

## integration simplex

A simplex or role by which a tensor configuration is instantiated as object-whole at a native scale.

## legible

Structurally registrable. Legible does not require consciousness.

## observer

A simplex performing registration. Observer does not necessarily mean mind.

## question

A bounded unresolved energy-state.

## readable

Structurally available to registration. Readable does not require understanding.

## registration

The capacity of a simplex to preserve, express, or transmit sequential tensor alteration.

## relation

Readable configuration within tensor.

## scalar

Held energy-state.

## simplex

A bounded state-bearing object. A simplex holds or modifies energy in a state of being.

## story

Conscious registration of time.

## tensor

Primitive simultaneous arrangement of energy-states.

## time

Sequential tensor alteration.

## transformation

Vector-compelled alteration of energy-state.

## vector

Motioned energy-state. Energy-state vectors alter energy-state scalars.
