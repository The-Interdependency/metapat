# Example: Registration

## Purpose

The registration example tests whether METAPAT remains mind-agnostic.

## Statement

Time is sequential tensor alteration.

Registration is the capacity of a simplex to preserve, express, or transmit sequential tensor alteration.

An observer is a simplex performing registration.

An observer need not be conscious.

## Non-conscious registration examples

A sedimentary rock face may register sequential alterations through layered deposits.

A hard drive may register sequential alterations through written byte-blocks.

A sensor stream may register sequential alterations through ordered readings.

A downstream code module may register sequential alterations through changed state.

None of these requires consciousness.

## Conscious registration example

A human mind may register time as memory, expectation, causal narration, or story.

Story is conscious registration of time.

Story is not time itself.

## Guardrail

Do not say:

```text
Consciousness creates time.
```

Say:

```text
Time is sequential tensor alteration.
Consciousness may register time as story.
```
